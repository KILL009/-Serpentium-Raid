Getting Started:

This raid comes in four different difficulties (D1~D4) with D4 being unavailable at the time this guide was made. It uses the regular 6 man Raid Party and has a 95% Demon Realm debuff as well as a 30% Crit debuff. The 2nd dungeon of the raid also doesn’t use mechs with a timed CD but rather consists of HP% triggers, meaning you can’t skip any of them as the boss will become invulnerable.

Additionally this raid has weekly clear drops and repeatable drops unlike previous raids.

For the guide itself, attacks written in the color red ignore all i-frames.

Serpentium Tower: Security System Core  (20-4-1)

(IMPORTANT FOR BOTH PHASES) Activate Annihilation Mode:

Both bosses have 1 Freeze and 1 Petrify count with a 90s CD. (200 Light/Water res)

Every time you are hit by any attack, you’ll gain one Surveillance debuff which lasts 15s, cannot be cleansed and can stack up to 20 times. This can rapidly build up due to the boss also having lots of fast multi-hits.

Once any player reaches 20 stacks the boss will instantly cast the following attack and all stacks will be removed from the debuffed player.

(^) Annihilation Mode:

The boss will instantaneously reveal a mapwide random laser pattern for a few seconds before the actual attack goes off. The real lasers are slightly bigger than the attack indicator lines and hurt quite a lot. However the lasers can be avoided by using i-frames

Example in phase 1 from this video.

(^) Tighten Security Measures:

When the boss drops below 50% HP, its Attack Power will be increased and if it hasn’t ulted yet it will automatically trigger one of its ults at this HP threshold.

Regular Attacks:

Electromagnetic Wave Release:

The Boss surrounds itself with short ranged circular waves which inflict random cleansable debuffs (e.g: confusion, aging) if you get hit by this attack.

Sometimes the boss instantly uses this attack at the start (video).

Electromagnetic Ring Release:

Similar to the last attack, the boss releases a ring around it which deals continuous damage. However you can easily dodge this by hugging the boss. And unlike the previous attack this ring only gives you a cleansable Attack Decrease debuff.

(video)

Charge:

The boss quickly charges in a direction (up, left, right or down), leaving a lingering area of constant multihit pulses behind. Getting hit by this will give you a cleansable Leg Injury debuff (no dashing).

(video)

Charging Rod Laser Release:

The boss will release red horizontal lasers from the Charging Rod’s positions on the map which will cover the map horizontally. Getting hit by this will inflict a cleansable Confusion debuff.

(video)

Electric Discharge:

Flowing electricity will zap one of the two floors depending on the color of the attack. Red electricity will always linger on the ground floor. Whereas yellow electricity will linger on the 1st floor.

Red electricity example from this video.

Targeted Missile:

The boss will track one player's location with a golden crosshair before confirming a position where a missile will shortly strike. The explosion damage is high so if you’re the marked player, it’s best to lead the marked location away from your party.                                     PR is the target here (video)

Lightning Discharge:

Randomly shoot either 1, 3 or 5 condensed lightning lasers in fixed positions from above. The lasers hurt a ton so it's best to consider dodging it when you see the message at the top of the screen counting down “5.. 4.. 3..” indicating this attack will happen.

The 3 laser version will lose the outermost lasers and the single laser version is just the middle laser (video).

Ultimates:

Charging Mode (wipe mech):

This attack ignores all resurrection effects.

4 Charging Rods will appear on the map and the Boss will link itself to one and charges, which restores 1% of its total HP every 5s. If the boss remains in this charging state for a minute, it’ll wipe the party.

To stop the boss from charging the party must deal 10% of the boss’ HP at the time it enters charging mode. (e.g: Boss enters Charging Mode at 200x, if you inflict 20 HP bars of damage it’ll stop). If you walk up to a Charging Rod and interact with it using the Dungeon Special Function key, you’ll gain a 10% Attack buff for 10s to help with this.

When the Boss exits Charging Mode, an indicator will appear above the Charging Rod it was connected to, which lets you turn it off.

Keep in mind the boss can still attack in this state (video).

Circuit Overload:

This attack ignores all resurrection effects.

The boss will teleport to a random location on the right side of the map and idle. A glowing Charging Rod will be spawned in a random position on the left side of the map. Touching the red glowing area emitted from this Rod or from another player will give you the “Bind” buff and once you reach 5 stacks you’ll be stuck in place with the circuit continuing to emit in the direction your character is facing (left/right).

The person who links up with the charging rod will have the circuit emit in all 4 directions (up/down/left/right), using this the other players can take turns adding onto the circuit in order to have the red energy flow to where the boss is, overloading it.

You can lower the vertical height of the circuit by jumping up to get buffed as the energy will also emit in a circle around a player. (Look at SU (video))

Successfully passing this ult will make the boss groggy and when it's hit by 3 Hyperactives, it’ll stay groggy for longer.

System Restart (wipe mech):

This attack ignores all resurrection effects, it is also the longest ult of the 3.

The boss will teleport to the center of the ground floor and remain idle. Multiple Charging Rods will spawn on the map, players must connect them to the boss by interacting with the rods and then making the line pass through the boss. A single player can interact with multiple rods at once too.

Once the boss is hooked up to all the rods, blue spheres will descend from the center of the map. Players must grab one and use it to turn off a rod. If a player absorbs two spheres without getting rid of the previous one by turning off a charging rod, they’ll instantly die. Due to this it’s best to have one person as a backup for this.

You can watch this video up to 5:03 for the full mech as it’s quite long.

If the boss absorbs one orb, it’ll recover 10% HP and if it absorbs two, it’ll wipe the party. When successfully cleared, the boss will enter the groggy state which can be extended if 3 or more Hyperactives are used on it.

Serpentium Tower: Defense System (20-4-2)

Remember that the “important for both phases” stuff applies here too.

The map for this phase is in the air, you will die if you fall off the sides of the map.

A lot of the attacks overlap in this phase, making it quite chaotic.

If these hit a player it’ll simply deal a low amount of damage. However, making these Cubes hit the boss will “charge” it. After the boss has been hit by 15 of these cubes, it’ll enter the groggy state which can be extended by using 3 Hyperactives.

When the boss’ HP reaches 50%, the charging progress will be reset to 0/15.

The one on the very left is a randomly fired one, they sometimes happen (video).

Ultimates:

Area Scan - Paired Matching Ver:

This attack ignores all resurrection effects.

The boss moves to the back of the map and every player will be given a symbol (blue/yellow/green). One color will be assigned to two party members who must pair up and find the interactable nodes with the same color as them. When both players interact with the nodes they stand on, a barrier of the same color will appear and keep them undetectable as the boss scans the entire map once.

Do not activate the barrier too early as they will deactivate after some time has passed. If a single player is detected in the scan, the entire party will be wiped.

(video)

If you don’t have a partner, you can attempt to put up your barrier on  your own.

However you can also quickly jump off the side of the map and die before the scan occurs to prevent wipes.

Area Scan - Mass Ver:

This attack ignores all resurrection effects.

The boss moves to the back of the map and reveals 3 symbols (blue/yellow/green) in a random order. All players must stand in the colored barriers as instructed by the symbol appearance order as the boss scans the area three times.

E.g: boss shows yellow -> green -> blue symbols   -> players hide in yellow barrier as boss scans once, then in the green barrier for the 2nd scan and finally in the blue barrier for the last scan.

Again if one player is detected by the boss, the entire party will be wiped.

Video example, watch until 6:30.

Orchestra Hall: The Conductor (20-5-1)

(IMPORTANT FOR BOTH PHASES) Music notes:

You can freeze and petrify 20-5-1 once every 90s and only while it’s idle (not attacking). Both bosses have 500 resistance towards every element. 20-5-2 I have no information on for the time being.

In this dungeon, three kinds of music notes will be present in almost every HP% trigger mech so it’s best to know how to tell them apart from each other.

♩ = Quarter Note: use 1 or Z to refer to these

♪ = Eighth Note: use 2 or X to refer to these

𝅘𝅥𝅯 = Sixteenth Note: use 3 or C to refer to these


This being an example of both the colors and keys
Already being assigned in-game.

Artist’s Block, Dissolutions and Inspiration:

Whenever you are hit by the Conductor, you’ll gain one stack of the Pain of Creation debuff. Whenever you reach 3 stacks of this debuff it becomes the Source of Creation debuff (this one has the ! on its icon).

While the Source of Creation debuff is utterly harmless at first, getting hit by any of the three instrument attacks (“The ....” attacks) will cause you to enter the Dissolution state. While in this state you’ll “hold” the corresponding instrument based on the attack that hit you and have a marker with the Dungeon Special Function Key (default: F1) above your head, pressing this key will allow you to Dispel the instrument in your current location.

However if you gain another Source of Creation debuff (2 stacks), it’ll forcibly Dispel whatever instrument you hold. Additionally you will gain a stack of SoC if you’re hit by an ally’s Dispel too, although this won’t force a Dispel out of you it can end up in a forced 3 stack Dispel with disastrous effects.

You do NOT want to hit Conductor with Dispels as it will heal 5% of its HP. You want to safely use Dispels away from the Conductor while it’s still in its easily controllable 1 stack of Source of Creation state.

TLDR: have ! debuff and get hit by instrument attack -> run away from the party and hit F1 so the boss doesn’t get hit and heals

Dispel - Violin:

If you’re hit by “The Violinist” while in the Dissolution state, you’ll hold a violin.
When using F1 to Dispel this instrument, you’ll release a floor wide horizontal yellow laser that’ll heal 5% of the Conductor’s HP if it hits it.

  • (1 Stack) The laser is 1 row tall
  • (2 Stacks) The laser is now 3 rows tall.
  • (3 Stacks) The laser is now 5 rows tall (basically a guaranteed boss heal).

Single stack violin dispel (video) keep in mind it’ll always spawn on the platform's ground and not in the air as shown here (the boss healed 15x as the Dispel hit it).

Dispel - Cello:

If you’re hit by “The Cellist” while in the Dissolution state, you’ll hold a cello.

When using F1 to Dispel this instrument, you’ll release a yellow circular soundwave which continuously deals damage. Same heal applies here.


(2 & 3 Stacks) The sound wave has a higher duration and thus lingers longer.

It’s best to Dispel these in a corner away from everyone or as high up in the air as possible.

Dispel - Flute:

If you’re hit by “The Flutist” while in the Dissolution state, you’ll hold a flute.

When using F1 to Dispel this instrument, you’ll release a circular explosion that deals lethal damage, the radius of the explosion increases as time passes. Same heal applies here again.

(2 & 3 Stacks) The maximum size of the explosion increases.
⁢
If you have this run away from your party members IMMEDIATELY and use Dispel asap, forced Dispels of this instrument can easily result in instantaneous wipes.

For clarity: the faint RED circle off to the left side is MO’s Mod Eternal Fog. The golden circular explosions are the Dispel.

In this video you can see the entire process. BL has 2 stacks of Pain of Creation and gets hit by an attack -> 3 stacks of PoC turns into Source of Creation (!) and then BL gets hit by his ally’s Flute Dispel -> BL gains x2 Flute Dispel -> BL dies from his Dispel as he lost both his SoT and 19-4 title res.

Regular Attacks:

The Violinist:

The Conductor pulls out its violin and plays it, causing a horizontal laser to spawn at a random party member’s location. Getting hit by this will give you an uncleansable -10% Physical/Magical Attack debuff that lasts for 10s.

The Cellist:

The Conductor pulls out its Cello and plays it, causing a red circle to spawn at its location which will suddenly burst and pulse with electromagnetic waves which deal a single hit if you don't move out of its attack range, otherwise it can deal two hits.

Baton swing:

The Conductor swings his baton and strikes the ground, leaving an explosion in its wake. This can stack up the Pain of Creation debuff rather fast.

Glowing Notes:

This attack ignores all resurrection effects.

The conductor spawns in a random pattern of various notes which are colored in Blue/Purple/Red. Notes must be hit by a skill of the corresponding type (Blue = Tenacity, Purple = Strength, Red = Bravery) in order to make them glow just before the golden vertical line moves past them. If the golden line touches any non glowing note, a hard hitting map-wide explosion will occur. More notes spawn in the lower the Conductor’s HP is. When successfully cleared, the Conductor will be groggy after this mech.

Successful Glowing Notes clear, CC uses Wonder Wall to clear his tenacity note (video).

Note Check:

This attack ignores all resurrection effects.

When this mech starts a 7x4 box of orbs with the 3 various music notes spawn in and every player is assigned a number from 1~6 above their head. Every vertical row will go in order of ♩ ►♪►𝅘𝅥𝅯.

You must stand on the note according to your assigned number and the row the note is on according to the sheet music. When in place do not move at all to avoid having your placement fail, only move AFTER your note has been checked off on the sheet music.

The Conductor will teleport to players in order and check their placements, while this happens the player being checked cannot move. After someone has used an Orb, said Orb will be unavailable for a few seconds meaning if there are 2 of the same notes in the same row (e.g row 3 ♩, ♩) after each other, the next person needs to stand on the OTHER Orb in the same row.

250x Sonata example from this video.

The number of notes depends on which HP trigger this mech occurs on (6/8/10/12) and when it happens on the 10x trigger, a repeat sign ( :|| ) will appear after the 7th note. This means the entire order has shifted to 2 > 3 > 4 > 5 > 6 > 1 > 2 from the start to the end.

Here’s a video example of this mech done with one mistake at 10x, watch until 7:20.

One incorrect placement will result in the Conductor not becoming groggy after the mech and two wrong placements will cause the party to be wiped.

Tuning (wipe mech):

This attack ignores all resurrection effects.

A red vertical line will form on the left side of the map which pulls players towards it. A music score will appear under the Conductor’s HP bar and orbs with all 3 music note variations will form on the right and slowly make their way over to the line. The orbs must reach the line in the order according to the music score. When hitting an orb it’s shape changes (♩ ►♪►𝅘𝅥𝅯 ► removed) use this to your advantage to successfully clear this mech.

If one incorrect orb is read, the Conductor recovers 5% of its HP and it won't be groggy afterwards. Should two incorrect orbs be read, the party will be wiped. When successfully cleared, the Conductor will be groggy after this mech.

Orchestra Hall: Maestro (20-5-2)

Here the party will face off against Maestro, Maestro has 4 hitboxes (left hand, right hand, head and body) and is a background boss like Malice 12-7-1 and Berserk Berthe 15-5-2.

Dissonance:

The Dissonance gauge is present throughout the entirety of Phase 2, this SHOULD be client side, however multiple desyncs are possible when it comes to key locks. It goes up by 6% every time you get hit by an attack and can be lowered by successfully clearing several mechs. It can also increase by making mistakes during certain mechs.

The Dissonance gauge is located above Maestro’s HP bar.

When the gauge reaches 100%, one of your consumable or skill buttons will be rendered unusable for the rest of the dungeon unless you successfully pass certain mechs to restore the keys. You can however change what skill or potion is in the slot.

The key blocks will go in order from: 1 → Q →  2 →  A

Regular Attacks

Melody Lasers:

Maestro fires Melody Laser either horizontally or vertically. These can be tanked but should be dodged as every hit will increase the Dissonance gauge by 6%.

When Maestro’s HP is below 225x, the lasers will fire much faster.

A lone Melody Laser with the sub 225x HP speed. (Video)

Accelerando:

Maestro fires off a fast barrage of Melody Lasers which increase the Dissonance gauge by 3% every time one hits a player. The barrage also has cymbals spawning near Maestro’s head which deals more damage than a singular laser, hits the entire map and increases the Dissonance gauge by 6%. Try your best to avoid as many hits as possible to avoid ramping up the Dissonance gauge.


 An Accelerando taken from this video.

Orb Drop:

Maestro will summon 3 blue and 2 gold vertical pillars of music which indicate where orbs of the same color will land. Having a gold orb hit you will inflict fatal damage and also restore the last locked skill key/pot key, however having a blue orb hit you seems to only deal minor damage. Make sure to have a res up if you’re going to take a gold orb. Additionally, a Choir Member will be summoned for every orb that doesn’t hit a player or a summon (e.g: Gurumi).

Only 3/5 orbs hit players/summons-> 2 Choir Members spawn (video).

All 5 orbs hitting a player/summon resulting in no Choir Members spawning (video).

Choir Member Summon: 

Maestro will fire a vertical Melody Laser and a Choir Member will spawn in its location. Choir Members let out an aura around them which increases skill cooldowns of all party members regardless of where you are on the map and the Dissonance gauge by 2% per tick. If only one Choir Member is on the field they will become enraged.

Hyperactives will also instantly kill any Choir Member hit by them.


CC instantly deletes them by pressing his HA (video).

You have to press the Z X C keys in time with the notes on the screen (like Taiko no Tatsujin/other Rhythm games). The Dissonance gauge increases by +30% for every failed note and decreases by 20% for every successful note.

Ensemble:

This mech ignores all resurrection effects.
Full example of a successfully cleared Ensemble (watch until 14:10).

All players are transformed into musical instruments and must play the exact notes in the right order shown by an instrument in the background playing the notes before you. Again ♩ = 1/Z,  ♪ = 2/X, 𝅘𝅥𝅯 = 3/C.

Players will always go in order based on the party join order in the positions below (1=host etc).

For the first time this mech occurs players will have 6 notes to play and on the 90x trigger this will be increased to 8. You can type the notes as they show up in party chat or whatever chat you use e.g: CCXXZX and then play these notes in the same order.


One example taken from this video.

If all players successfully clear this mech, the Dissonance gauge will be reset back to 0% and Maestro will be groggy for a while. If you successfully perform all of your notes, you’ll have your most recent unusable skill key/pot restored.


Incorrect notes however will increase the dissonance gauge and will end up killing you once your turn is over. You will also die if you don’t input 6 notes too. Maestro will also recover 5% of its HP for every player who dies during this mech. This mech will automatically WIPE the party if there are 3 or less players alive at any time during it.

Possibly a bug: swapping titles during this mech also causes you to die.

The Dissonance gauge seems to decrease by 30% on correct notes and increases by 27% on the first incorrect note and 50% on every incorrect note afterwards.
This however needs more research for the exact details.

Encore:

TLDR: this is Maestro’s “Berserk” deal with this and then you can clear.


Example taken from this video.

Maestro heals back to 45x HP and starts to rapidly attack with lots of Melody Lasers with additional cymbals that spawn in the location of every party member here and there. The lasers hurt a lot more compared to before. While this happens just focus on dodging as much as you can and avoid stacking up together as the cymbals can easily wipe the entire party.

After his outburst Maestro will be groggy for a short while which can be extended by using your Hyperactive. This is where players can finally burst it down and clear the raid. If Maestro is still alive once the groggy state ends, he will simply repeat this process until either it or the party is dead.
